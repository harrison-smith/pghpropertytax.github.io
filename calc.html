Overtaxation Calculator for Homes Purchased after Jan. 1, 2013
Throughout this report we use assessment-to-sale ratios to measure how property taxes are levied on homeowners in the City of Pittsburgh. To see if you’re paying more than your fair share, follow the directions below:
Step 1: Find assessment data for your home
Go to the County Assessor's website and search your address.
Step 2: Determine your home's assessed value
Scroll to the bottom of the page and locate “2021 Full Base Year Market Value.” The “Total Value” is the dollar amount at which the county has assessed your property. Because Allegheny County relies on a base year system, barring catastrophic circumstances, the assessed value of your property was fixed in 2012. Enter this number in the top box of the calculator.
Note: Scroll back to the top. If next to “Homestead” in the first column, it says “No,” the homestead exemption has not yet been applied to your property. If it is your primary residence, you may be eligible to decrease the taxable value of your property by $18,000.
